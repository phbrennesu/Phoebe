Purebreds

Purebreds are dogs with a documented pedigree. According to their website, the American Kennel Club, also known as AKC, "manages and registers purebred dogs" The American Kennel Club has been the leader in dog breeds for 135 years. They offer expert advice for dog owners and many ways to get involved. They also help potential dog owners find a pet that is right for them and their lifestyle. The organization is a great resource for information about purebred dogs, training, nutrition, and health. The AKC divides purebred dogs into seven categories. These categories compete yearly for best in show.

This year, at the 2019 National Dog Show, the Havanese won the Toy Group, The Siberian Husky won the Working Group. The Pharaoh Hound won the Hound Group. The Old English Sheepdog won the Herding Group. The Soft Coated Wheaten Terrier won the Terrier Group. A Bulldog named Thor won best in show. Thor won his title against 2,000 other dogs representing 194 breeds. The shows are a great way for dog owners to come together The show was extremely competitive this season and continues to grow more competitive each year.

The AKC

The American Kennel Club was founded in 1884. AKC's goal is to "manage and register purebred dogs" The AKC categorizes dogs into eight different groups. These groups are:

  1. Herding Group
  2. Sporting Group
  3. Toy Group
  4. Working Group
  5. Hound Group
  6. Non-Working Group
  7. Terrier Group
  8. Miscellaneous Group

This year, at the 2019 National Dog Show, the Havanese won the Toy Group, The Siberian Husky won the Working Group. The Pharaoh Hound won the Hound Group. The Old English Sheepdog won the Herding Group. The Soft Coated Wheaten Terrier won the Terrier Group.

A Bulldog named Thor won best in show. Thor won his title against 2,000 other dogs representing 194 breeds. The shows are a great way for dog owners to come together The show was extremely competitive this season and continues to grow more competitive each year.

Popular Hybrids

Hybrids have grown in popularity over the years because they combine different traits that people love in a breed. For example, a golden doodle combines the friendly nature of a golden retriever with the intelligence and hypoallergenic coat of a poodle. The most popular hybrid dog breeds according to dog.com are:

  1. Cockapoo
  2. Labradoodle
  3. Goldendoodle
  4. Maltese Shih Tzu
  5. Puggle
  6. Schnoodle
  7. Yorkipoo

Breeder Wally Conron first created the labradoodle because he was looking to create a guide dog for a women whose husband was allergic to dogs. He said that he now feels a sense of responsibility for the growth of hybrid dogs especially from unethical breeders who are unaware of what they are doing. The importance of a good breeder is important no matter the type of dog. Always make sure to do your research when you are getting a dog from a breeder.

Mutts

Mutts are mixes of different breeds and cannot be identified as one breed. Mutts do not belond to one offically recognized breed. Most Americans believe that Purebreds are the most popular dog, but in fact Mutts are the most commonly own dog in the United States. Angela Hughes, veterinary genetics research manager at Mars Veterinary explains Mutt trends, “the DNA of America’s mixed-breed dogs tells a story of which breeds were popular in past decades. If a breed was trendy in the past, but has fallen from popularity, it may still represent a large portion of the current mixed-breed population.”

Why adopt a mutt? By adopting a mutt, you are saving a life. Mutt dogs are also reported to have more diverse personalities. Your mutt dog will be more unique in comparison to purebred and hybrid dogs. Mutts are also more resistant to genetic diseases. This means that mutts live longer and are stronger than their furry friends. A mutt can make a great addition to your family! About Helping Hounds

Helping Hounds Dog Rescue is a non-profit organization located in Syracuse, NY. The organization's mission is to help dogs in over crowded shelters find loving homes. Before adopting a dog, Helping Hounds says you should consider the following:

  1. Can you make a 10 to 15 year financial commitment?
  2. Who will care for your dog when you are not at home?
  3. Is anyone in your household allergic to pets?
  4. Can you walk, feed, and train your dog?

Rescuing a dog can have many benefits on the dog and the owner. Often, people who rescue dogs jokingly ask themsevles "Who rescued who?" It can be especially important to look out for older dogs in shelters becaue they are often overlooked for younger puppies. Older dogs can be just as fullfilling as puppies and often need less training. By adopting a dog from a shelter, you are saving animals from being euthanized. Adopting a dog from a shelter also opens up more space for the shelter to care for more animals.

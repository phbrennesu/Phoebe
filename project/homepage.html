History of Dogs

According to ABC News and Sweedish Genecist Pontus Skoglund, the relationship between humans and dogs began as long as 40,000 years ago! Scientists like Skoglund believe that dogs became attracted to humans because of the scraps of food that they left behind. It is easy to believe that not much has changed since then! My dog Kori loves eating scraps from the table. There is more that explains the love between a human and a canine. When a dog and a person make eye contact, the hormone oxytocin is released. Oxytocin can help people and pets strengthen their bonds.
According to National Geographic, all dogs can be traced back to a wolf. Evidence of such is seen through the way domestic dogs are territoral and communication through scent. Besides scent, National Geographic reports that dogs also use their body language to communciate. This can include when a dog wags its tail or when it barks. Besides being a best friend, dogs can work in fields, police forces, and security.
